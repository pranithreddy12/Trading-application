Section I — Executive Summary

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Shah Quantum Fund ('SQF' or 'the Fund') is commissioning the design and construction of ATLAS — the Autonomous Trading & Learning Agent System — a production-grade, self-improving, multi-agent trading infrastructure operating across five asset classes: US equities, US options, US futures, cryptocurrency, and foreign exchange, with a sixth division for prediction markets.

ATLAS is not a trading bot. It is an organism of 90+ specialized AI agents organized in seven operational layers plus a Scout Network, coordinated by a Meta-Orchestrator, with a self-improvement loop that makes the system measurably better every day it operates. The system ingests data from the fastest and most granular sources available — including Level 2 market data and order flow — stores, enriches, and enhances that data, triggers strategy entries and exits in real time, generates novel strategies autonomously, validates them with institutional-grade rigor, and feeds all outputs into a growing data backend designed to train machine learning models at scale.

The target: produce thousands of validated strategies by year-end, with the top performers deployed to live capital. The compounding data advantage ATLAS accumulates daily makes the system uncatchable after six months of operation.

Section III — ATLAS System Architecture

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

3.1  Architecture Philosophy

ATLAS is built on five architectural principles that govern every design decision in this document.

Principle 1 — Agents Communicate Across Levels, Not Just Within

Every agent has both vertical outputs (to the next layer in the pipeline) and horizontal outputs (to agents in other layers that benefit from its signal). A Data Collector does not merely hand data to a Feature Engineer — it simultaneously signals Strategy Ideators when anomalies appear, and alerts Risk Controllers when data feeds degrade. This cross-level mesh creates compound intelligence: the output of the whole system exceeds the sum of individual agents.

Principle 2 — Agents Spawn Agents

The Meta-Orchestrator does not manage a fixed roster. When backtest volume exceeds capacity, it spawns additional Backtest Runner instances. When a new asset class is added, it spawns the corresponding Data Collectors, Feature Engineers, and Ideators automatically. The system scales by multiplication, not by manual configuration.

Principle 3 — The Data Backend Is a First-Class Product

Every data point ATLAS touches — raw market data, enhanced features, strategy performance, agent behavior, validation results, execution quality, scout intelligence — is stored, indexed, and available for ML training. The data backend is not an afterthought; it is one of the system's three primary outputs alongside trading P&L and strategy generation. Within 12 months, this database will be the largest proprietary repository of enhanced, quality-verified trading data and strategy performance data in existence. It is the fuel for machine learning models that will compound ATLAS's advantage indefinitely.

Principle 4 — Every Layer Self-Improves

Self-improvement is not limited to the Meta-Learning layer. Every layer feeds performance data back to its own agents and to the Meta-Learner. Data Collectors track their own latency and accuracy. Ideators track which hypothesis patterns produce validated strategies. Validators track their false-positive and false-negative rates. Execution agents track slippage and optimize routing. The entire system is a learning organism, not a static pipeline.

Principle 5 — Security Through Lifecycle Management

Agents that operate on trader laptops (the Fidelity Monitoring Agents) follow strict lifecycle rules: they activate at market open, monitor during trading hours, and are killed at market close. Only the cloud-based data feed agent persists overnight, collecting news and pre-market data to generate opening hypotheses. This architecture ensures that no sensitive trading data persists on local machines outside of active trading hours.

Section IV — Layer 1: Data Ingestion, Order Flow & Enrichment

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

22 agents. The sensory system. Everything ATLAS knows about the world flows through this layer. The quality, speed, and granularity of data ingestion determines the ceiling of every other layer.

4.1  Design Imperative: Level 2 & Order Flow First

ATLAS is not built on delayed OHLCV bars. The primary data sources are Level 2 market data (full order book depth) and order flow (time & sales, trade prints, volume at price). These are the fastest and most accurate representations of market state available to non-HFT participants. All other data sources (OHLCV, news, sentiment, macro) are supplementary.

⚠  Level 2 and order flow data generate significantly higher data volumes than OHLCV. TimescaleDB hypertable partitioning and Redis hot-layer caching are essential to prevent the database from becoming the bottleneck. Size the infrastructure accordingly — see Section XIV.

4.6  The Enhanced Data Backend (ML Training Store)

Every data point that enters L1 — raw, enriched, and enhanced — is stored in a purpose-built backend designed for future ML model training. This is not a log archive. It is a structured, indexed, queryable data warehouse that will become the most valuable asset ATLAS produces after trading P&L itself.

Raw market data: Level 2 snapshots, order flow, OHLCV (all timeframes), options chains, futures term structure — partitioned by date and symbol in TimescaleDB hypertables.

Enhanced features: the full 400+ feature library, computed and stored at every timestep, in PostgreSQL + Redis.

Strategy performance: every strategy hypothesis, its code, backtest results, validation outcomes, paper/live P&L — all linked to the market conditions at the time. This is the training data for 'what strategies work in what regimes.'

Agent behavior: every agent action, decision, and error — linked to outcomes. This is the training data for 'what agent behaviors correlate with profitability.'

Scout intelligence: every external hypothesis sourced, its origin, its validation outcome. This is the training data for 'which external sources produce real alpha.'

Execution data: every order, fill, slippage measurement, latency — linked to market conditions at execution time. This is the training data for 'optimal execution under different conditions.'

Schema design: star schema with fact tables (trades, signals, features, agent_events) and dimension tables (strategies, agents, instruments, regimes). Optimized for analytical queries and batch ML training jobs. See Appendix D for full schema.

⚠  Data volume estimate: with Level 2 + order flow for 200+ US equities, ATLAS will generate approximately 50-100 GB of raw data per trading day. TimescaleDB compression (typically 10-20x) reduces storage needs, but the infrastructure must be sized accordingly. Budget for 2 TB storage in Month 1, scaling to 10 TB by Month 6.

Section V — Layer 2: Strategy Generation Engine

The philosophy: everything is possible until proven otherwise by data. But every hypothesis is tested with uncompromising statistical discipline before a single dollar is deployed.

The 'Mad Scientist' Protocol

Each Ideator agent is configured with an aggressive exploration mandate:

Temperature: set high (0.9-1.0) for initial hypothesis generation — explore widely.

Constraint: every hypothesis must include explicit mathematical risk parameters (stop loss, position size formula, max drawdown limit, regime filter). Creativity in signal; discipline in risk.

Volume target: each Ideator produces 10-20 hypotheses per day. Across 7 Ideators, that is 70-140 hypotheses daily, 500-1,000 weekly.

Mutation integration: every 5th hypothesis is a deliberate mutation of a previously validated strategy — change one parameter, one filter, one universe constraint, and re-test.

Failure analysis: every Ideator receives a daily feed from L7 of the top 10 failure patterns from yesterday's rejected strategies. The prompt: 'Do not generate strategies that would fail for these reasons.'

▸  The 'mad scientist' approach works because the cost of generating a bad hypothesis is near-zero (one Claude API call, ~$0.01-0.05). The cost of missing a profitable strategy is infinite opportunity cost. Generate aggressively, validate ruthlessly.

Fidelity Monitoring Agent — Detailed Specification

The Fidelity Monitoring Agent (L6-FM-01) is the manual trading side's connection to ATLAS. One instance runs on each Fidelity trading laptop. Its responsibilities:

Capture every order entry, fill, modification, and cancellation in real-time

Cross-reference each trade against ATLAS's own data stream (Level 2 + order flow from L1) to identify whether the trade aligns with any ATLAS strategy signals

Log all trading activity to the cloud backend for the Enhanced Data Store (feeding ML training)

Monitor for compliance: position size limits, drawdown limits, unauthorized symbol trading

Generate post-session trade journal automatically: every trade with entry/exit times, P&L, notes on market conditions

Lifecycle Management (Security Protocol)

PRE-MARKET: The Meta-Orchestrator spawns L6-FM-01 instances at 9:25 AM ET (5 minutes before market open). Each instance connects to its assigned laptop and begins monitoring.

MARKET HOURS: Full monitoring active. All data streams live.

POST-MARKET: At 4:05 PM ET (5 minutes after close), each L6-FM-01 instance uploads final session data to cloud, then self-terminates. No monitoring agent runs on any laptop outside market hours.

OVERNIGHT: Only L6-FM-02 (cloud agent) persists. It collects overnight news, pre-market data, and generates the opening hypothesis document for the next trading day.

NEXT MORNING: Meta-Orchestrator spawns fresh L6-FM-01 instances at 9:25 AM ET. Clean lifecycle. No stale state.

⚠  This lifecycle ensures no sensitive trading credentials or real-time position data persists on local machines outside market hours. All persistent data lives in the encrypted cloud backend only.

The Self-Improvement Compound Loop
